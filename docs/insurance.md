# Insurance

This document outlines the public liability insurance arrangements for **The Palimpsest Path** community arts project on the Port Cygnet Boardwalk.

## Requirement from Huon Valley Council

Huon Valley Council requires the Permit Holder to maintain public liability insurance for the event with a minimum financial value of **20 million AUD**.

This is supplied as a **Certificate of Currency** as part of the Community Event Permit supporting documents.

## Scope of Cover

### Insured party

- **Named insured**: (to be updated once confirmed – e.g., Peter Shanks as individual member, or Cygnet Arts Council).  
- Cover applies to the member’s approved performing / exhibiting / teaching activities as specified on their Certificate of Currency.

### Sum insured

- Public liability limit: **30,000,000 AUD** any one occurrence.  
- This exceeds the Huon Valley Council minimum requirement of 20 million AUD.

### Activities covered

Under Duck for Cover, activities are grouped by category.

For this project the relevant activity is:

- **Category 1 – Visual Artist / Craft Artist (including drawing, painting)**.

Project‑specific interpretation:

- Creating ephemeral chalk drawings, symbols and dance‑step style trails on the boardwalk surface.  
- Facilitating audience participation, where walkers are invited to add their own chalk marks in response to prompts.  
- No permanent modification of the boardwalk; chalk (calcium carbonate) washes away naturally with rain as part of the palimpsest design.

The policy allows audience participation as part of a performance/activity, subject to normal duty of care and statutory compliance.

### Territorial and temporal limits

- Territorial limit includes **Australia**, covering activities on the Port Cygnet Boardwalk (Tasmania).  
- Policy period: 2025–2026 Duck for Cover scheme year; cover for this project applies between the approved start and end dates shown on the Certificate of Currency.

## Relationship to Council Permit

- The project operates under a Huon Valley Council **Community Event Permit** for Port Cygnet Boardwalk.  
- The Duck for Cover policy automatically notes the interests of employers, councils and venues, which allows Huon Valley Council to be listed as an interested party on the Certificate of Currency.  
- The Certificate of Currency is uploaded with the permit application as the required proof of public liability insurance.

## Risk Management Obligations

The policy requires members to take **reasonable care** in managing risks associated with their activities. This includes:

- Complying with relevant by‑laws, regulations and permit conditions imposed by Huon Valley Council.  
- Taking reasonable measures to prevent personal injury and property damage (e.g., non‑slip chalk practices, visibility of markings, clear communication that the surface may be temporarily marked).  
- Maintaining any equipment or materials (e.g., chalk buckets, signage) in satisfactory condition.

The project’s separate **Risk Assessment** document (see `docs/risk-assessment.md`) details specific controls for the boardwalk environment, such as slip‑resistance, weather considerations, and interactions with other path users.

## What Is Not Covered

Key relevant exclusions from the Duck for Cover Public Liability policy include:

- Stunts and high‑risk physical activities.  
- Teaching of sporting or fitness activities (e.g., yoga, zumba, martial arts).  
- Pyrotechnics, amusement rides, and animal‑based attractions (petting zoos, horses, etc.).  
- Activities undertaken without required permits or in breach of law.

The Palimpsest Path has been designed to avoid all excluded activity types and to operate only under an approved Huon Valley Council permit.
