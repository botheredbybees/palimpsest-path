# Project Brief: Chalk the Walk (Cygnet Boardwalk)

**Project Lead:** Peter Shanks
**Academic context:** FXA301 Arts in the Community, University of Tasmania
**Location:** Cygnet Boardwalk, Tasmania (wooden infrastructure over a sensitive waterway)

---

## 1. Project Vision

To transform a 100-metre section of the Cygnet Boardwalk from a transient thoroughfare into a "Therapeutic Landscape." The project uses asynchronous, ephemeral chalk narratives to reduce "parallel isolation" among regular walkers, fostering social capital and cognitive resilience.

---

## 2. Core Intervention Mechanics

**Medium:** Non-toxic, pH-neutral calcium carbonate chalk.

**Surfaces:** Vertical wooden bannisters and horizontal wooden/gravel walkways.

**Methodology:** The "Palimpsest Effect" — embracing the Tasmanian rain as a natural "reset" button that reduces aesthetic anxiety and encourages high-frequency, low-stakes participation.

**Key Activity:** Starting with "Dance Step" trails (tracing shoes to create old-time dance instructions), the project will then introduce and encourage further decoration by providing materials and examples.

---

## 3. Technical Infrastructure (The "Attention Tracker")

**Hardware:** Distributed ESP32 or Raspberry Pi Pico W units.

**Sensors:** Dual infrared (IR) beam counters at the start and end of the "gallery" section.

**Data Goal:** Establish a 3-week baseline of transit times and dwell times.

**Analysis:** Use pattern recognition to distinguish between joggers (fast), regular walkers (medium), and participants (slow/dwell).

**Ethics:** Privacy by Design — no cameras, no Wi-Fi sniffing, local SD logging only.

---

## 4. Strategic Alignment & Rationale

**Neuroscience:**
- Activation of the dopamine reward pathway (novelty of new stories)
- Activation of the cerebellum and mirror neuron system (dance step rehearsal)

**Psychology:**
- Shifting from a sympathetic (rushed) to a parasympathetic (contemplative) nervous system response

**Policy alignment:**
- National Centre for Creative Health (2024)
- Huon Valley Health and Wellbeing Strategy (Action Area: Inclusion and Interaction)

---

## 5. Evaluation Framework

**Quantitative:** Comparative analysis of baseline vs. intervention dwell times.

**Qualitative:** Analysis of the digital photo-narrative archive (captured via QR code).

**Economic:** Measuring Social Return on Investment (SROI) by documenting increased community connection.
