### **Mini-Project Brief: The Hopscotch Court (Cygnet Boardwalk)**
**Project Phase:** Weeks 1–8 (Continuous Play & Engagement)
**Project Lead:** Peter Shanks
**Location:** Cygnet Boardwalk (Horizontal Surface)
**Reference:** *Palimpsest Path Section 4.1: The Lowest Possible Door*

---

#### **1. Vision & Rationale**
While the Waltz Box Step (motor rehearsal) and the Hand-Chain (social identity) address specific cognitive and social goals, the **Hopscotch Court** serves as a "universal anchor." It is a cross-generational symbol of play that requires zero instructions. By placing it on a thoroughfare, we transform "walking" into "jumping"—a shift from a purely functional movement to an expressive, high-energy physical act that triggers immediate dopamine release and breaks the monotony of "parallel isolation."

#### **2. Execution Mechanics**
* **Surface:** Horizontal wooden planks.
* **Medium:** Thick-edged white calcium carbonate chalk.
* **Layout:** The "Standard 10" grid.



**Drawing Protocol:**
1.  **Baseline:** Align the central spine of the court with the direction of travel on the boardwalk.
2.  **Scale:** Each square should be approximately **45cm x 45cm** (fitting across roughly 3–4 standard boardwalk planks).
3.  **The Double-Step:** Ensure squares 4/5 and 7/8 are side-by-side to allow for the classic two-footed landing.
4.  **Numbering:** Use large, bold Sans-Serif numerals (1–10). Avoid "perfect" lines; the hand-drawn nature is part of the "Palimpsest Effect," signaling that anyone is allowed to add to it.

---

#### **3. Implementation & Evolution**

| Week | Activity | Goal |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Weeks 1–2** | **The Basic Grid** | Solid white outlines. Establish the court as a permanent fixture. |
| **Weeks 3–5** | **Color Infill** | Encourage walkers to "shade in" specific squares using the color chalk from the stations. |
| **Weeks 6–8** | **The "Rule" Prompt** | Add a small chalk prompt near square 10: *"Make a rule for square 7."* (e.g., 'Spin once', 'Say hello'). |

#### **4. Observation & Data (The "Dwell" Factor)**
The Hopscotch court is a primary driver for the **Raspberry Pi Pico W** sensor data (Section 3). 
* **Transit vs. Dwell:** A jogger will skip over the court (<10s), but a family or group of friends playing will create a significant "Dwell" event (>60s).
* **The "Success" Image:** Photographing a regular "serious" commuter (e.g., someone in work attire) spontaneously doing a single hop as they pass through.



---

#### **5. Strategic Signage & Safety**
* **Placement:** Position the court away from narrow "bottleneck" sections of the boardwalk to ensure those who choose not to play can still pass safely.
* **Friction:** Monitor the chalk density. If the surface becomes powdery/slick, use a damp cloth to "set" the chalk into the grain of the wood.
* **Message:** Near the start, add the simple text: *"Hopscotch — Just for Fun!"*
