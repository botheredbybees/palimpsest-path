### **Mini-Project Brief: The Hand-Chain (Cygnet Boardwalk)**
**Project Phase:** Weeks 3–6 (Community Co-Authorship)
**Project Lead:** Peter Shanks
**Location:** Cygnet Boardwalk (Vertical Bannister/Handrail)
**Reference:** *Palimpsest Path Section 4.2: The Participant Journey*

---

#### **1. Vision & Rationale**
The "Hand-Chain" is a tactile, asynchronous intervention designed to bridge "parallel isolation" by creating a physical record of community presence. Unlike the shoe-print trails, which focus on motor rehearsal (cerebellum), the Hand-Chain focuses on **identity and connection**. By overlapping outlines, the project visually demonstrates the "Social Glue" effect (WHO, 2019), transforming a transit rail into a collective narrative surface.

#### **2. Execution Mechanics**
* **Surface:** The vertical face of the wooden bannister (eye-level for adults, accessible for children).
* **Medium:** White calcium carbonate chalk (high contrast against dark timber).
* **Technique:** The "Overlap Trace." 

**The Placement Protocol:**
1.  **Contact:** Lay palm flat on the rail with fingers spread naturally and thumb extended.
2.  **Connection:** The wrist must overlap the previous hand in the chain by approximately **3 cm**.
3.  **Trace:** Use the side of the chalk for a soft, "fuzzy" outline that reduces aesthetic anxiety. 
4.  **Direction:** The chain can move horizontally, climb the posts, or dip—provided every hand "touches" the one before it.



---

#### **3. Implementation Timeline**

| Phase | Activity | Goal |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Week 3** | **The Seed** | Lead adds the first 3 hands to set the direction and "permission" to touch the rail. |
| **Week 4** | **Network Activation** | Brief 2–3 "regular" walkers to add their hands. This creates the "social proof" needed for others to join. |
| **Week 5–6** | **Narrative Shift** | Introduce the prompt inside the palms: *"What's your name for this stretch of water?"* |
| **Ongoing** | **The Reset** | After rain, photograph the cleared rail (the blank palimpsest) and re-seed 2 hands within 24 hours. |

---

#### **4. Critical Constraints & Safety**
* **Environmental:** Ensure no chalk dust falls directly into the sensitive waterway. Use the side of the chalk to minimize "crumble."
* **Tactile Safety:** Inspect the bannister for splinters before selecting the "Hand-Chain" zone.
* **Privacy:** The intervention remains anonymous. Participants are encouraged to add a "date" or a "word," but names are optional and discouraged if they compromise privacy (Section 7.5).

---

#### **5. Evaluation Data (The "Hoping For" Moment)**
The success of this mini-brief is measured by **Intergenerational Connectivity**.
* **Target Image:** A child’s small hand outline placed inside or overlapping an adult’s larger outline. 
* **Data Point:** Document the "length of the chain" vs. "days since rain" to measure the speed of community re-seeding.
