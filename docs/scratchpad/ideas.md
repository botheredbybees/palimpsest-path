# Palimpsest Path — Ideas Scratchpad

A holding space for ideas that aren't part of the current project scope but are worth keeping. These might become prompts, installations, extensions, or just stay here as inspiration.

---

## Butterfly wings chalk outline

Draw a large, beautiful butterfly on the boardwalk surface — wings spread wide, body at the centre, sized so that an adult or child lying down fits naturally into the body position with the wings extending around them. The words **PLACE CHILD HERE** are written inside the body outline. The invitation is implicit: lie down, become the butterfly, get someone to take your photo.

**Why it works:**
The butterfly template does several things at once. It is visually arresting from a distance and draws people in before they understand what it is. Once they do understand, the participation is physical, joyful, and social — you need someone else to take the photo, which means it generates connection as well as images. Children will do it immediately; adults need a moment but rarely resist.

The wings are the design challenge. They need to be beautiful enough to photograph well, simple enough to redraw after rain, and scaled for both children and adults (a wingspan of around 1.5 metres works for most body sizes). Monarch butterfly colouring — orange, black, and white — would read well in chalk on grey timber. A more abstract or stylised wing pattern might survive repeated redrawing more consistently than a naturalistic one.

**Practical notes:**
- Best positioned on a wider section of the boardwalk where there is room to lie down without blocking foot traffic
- Needs to be redrawn after each rain event — could become part of the daily site audit routine, or a specific weekly task
- The butterfly body outline could be drawn first as a template in permanent chalk marker, with the decorative wing pattern redrawn in soft chalk — this preserves the positioning even after heavy rain
- Natural photography prompt: "photograph someone becoming the butterfly" fits the therapeutic photography framing on the Participate page

**Possible weekly variation:**
Change the wing pattern each week — one week naturalistic monarch, one week abstract geometric, one week community-drawn (participants add their own details to the wings over the week). By the end of the week the wings carry the accumulated marks of everyone who passed through.

## Mermaid tail

Draw an elaborate fish tail on the boardwalk boards — scales, fins, the full thing — with **SIT HERE** at the wide end where the hips would be. Someone sits at the edge of the boards or on a low railing with their legs concealed behind the tail. Photographed from above or at a low angle, they become a mermaid.

Works especially well near the water's edge where the channel is visible in the background. Could be paired with a mirror-image version so two people can sit tail-to-tail. Iridescent colours (blues, greens, purples) photograph beautifully in chalk.

## Bird wings — standing version

The butterfly idea lying down, but vertical: two large outstretched bird wings drawn on a flat section of the bannister boards or a wall surface, with **STAND HERE** at the body position. The person stands in front, arms at their sides, and the wings extend from their back. Photographed straight on, they become a large bird — an eagle, a black swan, whatever the wings suggest.

Connects directly to the birdlife page. A black swan wing spread would be particularly apt given the town's name, and the black-on-grey-timber would photograph well.

## Chalk binoculars

Draw a large pair of binoculars on the railing or a flat surface, with eye-holes sized for a face. **LOOK THROUGH HERE** inside each lens. Inside each circle, draw something you might see if you were looking across the channel — a black swan, a heron, a distant shoreline. The person presses their face to the binoculars and someone photographs them "looking".

Connects to the birdwatching theme and to the project's mindful-noticing framing. The drawing inside the lenses could change each week to match the weekly bird prompt.

## Giant footprints

A trail of oversized footprints — human, bird, or fantastically large — leading from the entrance of the boardwalk section toward the chalk station. Each footprint is larger than a real foot, spaced at a stride that requires a small leap to follow. At the end, a final print with **YOUR TURN** beside it.

Could be the footprints of a particular bird (oystercatcher prints are very recognisable), or an unknown giant creature, or a running child. Works as a simple way to draw people into the space before they have decided to engage with the chalk station proper.

## The invisible friend

Draw a chalk figure sitting on the railing or a bench — a simple, friendly outline in the style of a paper doll — with a gap beside it sized for a real person to sit. No text needed; the empty space is the invitation. Someone sits beside the invisible friend and the photo does the rest.

The figure could be drawn with some personality — reading a book, looking out at the water, holding a coffee — to make the pairing feel like a moment rather than a prop. Low-key and quietly funny. Works well for adults who might feel self-conscious about the more elaborate installations.
