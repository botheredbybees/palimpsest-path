### **Mini-Project Brief: The Boardwalk Palimpsest (Collaborative Story)**

**Project Phase:** Weeks 6–8 (Peak Co-Authorship & Narrative Arc)
**Project Lead:** Peter Shanks
**Location:** Cygnet Boardwalk (Top face of the wooden bannister/handrail)
**Reference:** *Palimpsest Path Section 1.2 (Asynchronous Dialogue)*

---

#### **1. Vision & Rationale**
The "Collaborative Story" is the most advanced stage of the Palimpsest Path. It shifts the community from motor rehearsal (Waltz) and self-reflection (Pulse) into **Collective Meaning-Making**. By contributing a short phrase or sentence to a growing narrative, participants engage in a form of "asynchronous play" that reduces social isolation. Neurobiologically, this activates the **prefrontal cortex** and **language centers**, encouraging walkers to "mentalize" or imagine the thoughts of the contributors who preceded them (The Intersection of Literature and Cognitive Science, Section 4).

#### **2. Execution Mechanics**
* **Surface:** The top horizontal face of the wooden bannister.
* **Medium:** White or colored calcium carbonate chalk.
* **The "Link" Logic:** Each contribution must physically "touch" or align with the previous one, following the flow of the boardwalk.

**The Narrative Protocol:**
1.  **The Opening:** The Project Lead writes a "Story Starter" (a short, open-ended clause).
2.  **The Extension:** Walkers are invited to add **a word, a phrase, or one short sentence** that follows the logic of the previous entry.
3.  **The Break:** If the story reaches a physical dead-end (e.g., a post), the next walker can start a "New Chapter" on the other side.

---

#### **5. Evaluation & Qualitative Coding**
This exercise provides the primary data for the **Thematic Analysis Register (Section 9.3)**.
* **Coding Dimensions:**
    * **Cohesion:** Does the story make logical sense, or is it surrealist?
    * **Tone:** Is the community narrative hopeful, nostalgic, or humorous?
    * **Persistence:** How many "entries" are added between rain events?
* **Safety & Ethics:** Per **Section 7.4 (Risk Matrix)**, any prosocial but "incongruent" additions (e.g., scribbles) are to be left as part of the palimpsest. Only explicitly harmful text is to be spot-cleaned.

---

#### **6. Visual Representation**
The story should appear as a continuous ribbon of varying handwriting styles, capturing the "Thinking Hands" framework (Week 5 Deep Dive) where the haptic act of writing reinforces the connection to the physical environment.
