# Section 9: Evaluation Instruments

**Print-ready qualitative coding sheets & digital data log reference — use alongside Evaluation_Data.xlsx**

---

## 9.1 Instrument Overview

This section contains six evaluation instruments. The qualitative instruments (9.2–9.4) are designed for print and hand-completion during or after boardwalk visits. The quantitative instruments live in the companion spreadsheet, `Evaluation_Data.xlsx`, which contains three data-entry sheets and an SROI calculator.

| Ref | Instrument | Format | When Used | Purpose |
|-----|-----------|--------|-----------|---------|
| **9.2** | Photo-Narrative Weekly Coding Sheet | Print / hand-fill | Sunday evenings, Weeks 1–8 | Qualitative coding of chalk surface photos against six dimensions |
| **9.3** | Thematic Analysis Register | Print / hand-fill | From Week 4 onward | Running record of emergent narrative themes |
| **9.4** | QR Signage Plain Language Statement | Print-ready text | Phase 0 — print before Week 1 | Consent and privacy statement for boardwalk signage |
| **9.5** | Participant Photo Submission Consent | WordPress form text | Phase 0 — install on site | Consent text for QR gallery upload form |
| **xlsx** | Pico W Daily Log | Excel (digital) | Daily, Phase 0 – Week 8+ | Sensor data entry, walker classification, weekly aggregation |
| **xlsx** | Happiness Index Coding Log | Excel (digital) | Daily, Weeks 3–8 | Daily photograph coding, weekly summary, rain reset flags |

## 9.4 QR Signage Plain Language Statement

Print-ready text for the weather-resistant QR code signage installed at the gallery entrance and exit. Review wording with FXA301 supervisor before printing. Minimum font size 12pt on final sign.

---

> **THE PALIMPSEST PATH**
> *A community art project on the Cygnet Boardwalk*
>
> The chalk steps and drawings you see here are part of a community wellbeing project run by a University of Tasmania student. The materials are non-toxic mineral chalk (calcium carbonate or gypsum based) — the same chalk used on school blackboards. It washes away naturally with rain.
>
> **You are warmly invited to add to it.**
>
> Pick up some chalk from the station box and:
> - Follow the dance steps — or invent your own
> - Respond to this week's prompt
> - Add a drawing, a word, or a line connecting your story to someone else's
>
> By adding to this artwork, you are participating in a community art project. Your contribution may be photographed for a digital archive and academic reflection. No personal identifying information is collected.
>
> The sensors on this boardwalk count pedestrian movements using infrared beams only. No images, faces, or personally identifiable data are collected.
>
> **[QR CODE HERE]**
>
> sidewalkcircus.org — Full privacy details at sidewalkcircus.org/privacy
>
> Project Lead: Peter Shanks | FXA301 Arts in the Community, University of Tasmania

## 9.5 Participant Photo Submission Consent (WordPress Form Text)

Install this text in the WPForms upload form on sidewalkcircus.org/participate. The consent checkbox must be required before submission is accepted. Review wording with FXA301 supervisor before going live.

---

> **SHARE YOUR PHOTO — sidewalkcircus.org Gallery**
>
> Did you add something to the Palimpsest Path today? Share a photo of your contribution and it may appear in the community gallery.
>
> **Form fields:**
> - Your first name or a nickname *(optional)*
> - A caption for your photo *(optional — max 100 words)*
> - Upload photo *(JPG or PNG, max 5MB)*
>
> **Consent checkbox** *(required):*
>
> ☐ I consent to this photo being published on the Palimpsest Path gallery at sidewalkcircus.org and used in academic research related to this project. I confirm that I took this photo and that it does not contain images of identifiable individuals without their consent. I understand I can request removal at any time by contacting the project lead via the contact page.
>
> **Submission note** *(display after form):*
>
> Thank you for contributing to the Palimpsest Path. Your photo will be reviewed before publication. If you would like to request removal of your submission at any time, please use the contact form.
