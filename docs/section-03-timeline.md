# Section 3: Implementation Timeline

*8-Week Project Plan — Cygnet Boardwalk, Tasmania*

**Project Lead:** Peter Shanks | **Context:** FXA301 Arts in the Community, University of Tasmania

---

## Timeline Overview

The project unfolds across three distinct phases: a Pre-Project block (minimum 2 weeks) for permissions, procurement, and baseline data collection; an 8-week intervention period structured as a deliberate escalation from silent observation through full community co-authorship; and a post-project observation period to assess whether habits of interaction persist after materials are withdrawn. The Raspberry Pi Pico W infrared sensor array runs continuously from Phase 0 through to Week 8, providing an unbroken quantitative record spanning the full project arc.

Photo documentation is archived continuously via QR-linked gallery at sidewalkcircus.org. Baseline validity requires a minimum of 10 clean sensor-measurement days before Week 1 begins.

---

## Phase 0: Pre-Project Foundation — Minimum 2 Weeks Before Week 1

**Purpose**
- Obtain all necessary permissions and materials before any public-facing activity begins.
- Establish a clean baseline dataset: minimum 10 valid Pico W sensor measurement days.
- Install and validate sensor hardware with no chalk present — pure behavioural baseline.

**Council & Permissions**
- Submit proposal to Huon Valley Council: "Permit for Ephemeral Public Art."
- Emphasise non-toxic calcium carbonate chalk, water-soluble and biodegradable.
- Frame as health-promotion initiative aligned with Huon Valley Health and Wellbeing Strategy.
- Allow minimum 2 weeks for permit response — do not begin Week 1 without written approval.

**Procurement**
- Source bulk Earth-Safe calcium carbonate chalk: blues, greens, ochres (reflecting Cygnet environment).
- Fabricate or source weather-resistant chalk station boxes for bannister mounting.
- Print weather-resistant QR code signage (project goal, participation instructions, privacy/consent).
- Order Raspberry Pi Pico W units, IR beam sensors, SD logging modules, weatherproof housings.

**Site Audit**
- Walk the 60m section and identify specific Bannister Stations and Walkway Zones.
- Prioritise locations for maximum visibility and foot traffic.
- Confirm non-slip surfaces; document any existing wear or hazard areas.
- Install Pico W sensor units at start and end of gallery section.

**Pico W Baseline**
- Begin continuous IR logging immediately after sensor installation.
- Target: minimum 10 valid measurement days before Week 1.
- Log to local SD card only — no Wi-Fi, no camera, Privacy by Design.
- If hardware faults reduce valid days below 10, extend Phase 0 accordingly.

> ⚠ Do not proceed to Week 1 until baseline validity threshold is met.

**WordPress Setup**
- Configure sidewalkcircus.org gallery for QR-linked photo uploads.
- Set up page structure: About the Project, Weekly Archive, Participate.
- Test QR code scan → landing page flow before signage is printed.

## Week 1: The Silent Walk — The Trail Begins

The first week establishes the dance step trail through a staged, 2–3 day escalation. Each new element is added on top of the previous — the palimpsest begins accumulating from day one. White chalk only this week; no colour, no chalk station, no prompts. The steps are the only invitation.

### Days 1–2: The Footprint Trail

**Chalk Activity**
- Trace simple footprint pairs (left/right shoe outlines) in white chalk, spaced at a comfortable walking stride.
- The trail begins at the gallery entrance and leads toward the water end, ending with two footprints side by side facing outward toward the port — an invitation to stop and contemplate the view.
- Keep the trail minimal and deliberate. Each pair of prints should feel like a gentle suggestion, not a directive.

**Intent:** *Draw people toward the view. The final two prints facing the water are the project's first dwell invitation.*

### Days 3–5: The Tightrope Line

**Chalk Activity**
- Add a single chalk line running the full length of the trail.
- Place footprints along the line — some facing forward, some facing backward, all balanced on the line as if walking a tightrope.
- The trail now reads as a playful physical challenge superimposed on the footprint invitation.

**Intent:** *Disrupts gait. A walker who tries to walk the line is already participating. The reversed prints add humour and double-takes.*

### Days 6–7: The Hopscotch Court

**Chalk Activity**
- Add a hopscotch court (standard 10-square layout, ~45cm × 45cm per square) adjacent to the trail near the chalk station location.
- White outline only — no fill, no numbering yet.
- See `/docs/scratchpad/Week_1-8-hopscotch.md` for full layout and drawing protocol.

**Intent:** *Cross-generational recognition. Zero instructions needed. A serious commuter doing a single hop is a research success.*

**Observation (all week)**
- Continue unobtrusive observation of walker behaviour: stops, glances, speed changes.
- Note any spontaneous interactions prompted by the steps.

**Infrastructure**
- Install QR code signage at the gallery entrance and exit.
- Chalk station boxes installed but remain empty — visible as an invitation, not yet open.

> ⚠ Rain washout protocol applies throughout: re-apply within 24 hours, photograph before and after, log re-seed date in Pico W records. Post-washout engagement curves are valid research outcomes.

**No Prompt Yet** — *The trail is the only prompt. Let curiosity do the work.*

---

## Week 2: The Dance Steps Arrive — Full Trail Established

The second week completes the trail by adding the most complex element — the waltz box — and introduces colour to the existing elements. You remain the sole author. The chalk station opens at the end of this week, ready for Week 3.

### Days 8–10: The Waltz Box Steps

**Chalk Activity**
- Add the box waltz step sequence (6 numbered footprints in a closed loop, ~60cm × 60cm footprint) near the midpoint of the trail.
- Use the traced shoe template method: right foot template for steps 2, 4, 6; flipped for steps 1, 3, 5.
- Add a "Start Here →" arrow and small circled numbers (1)–(6) near the arch of each print.
- See `/docs/scratchpad/Week_1_Solo_Waltz_Box.md` for full layout and drawing protocol.

**Intent:** *The most cognitively engaging element — rewards repeat visits as walkers learn the sequence. A closed loop means no one has to go anywhere to try it.*

### Days 11–14: Colour and Consolidation

**Chalk Activity**
- Introduce colour: add fill to hopscotch squares, flourishes around footprints, decorative elements between elements.
- Add numbered squares (1–10) to the hopscotch court.
- Expand and reinforce the tightrope line as needed after rain events.
- You remain the sole decorator — building visual richness to model what is possible.

**Goal** — *Show walkers what's possible. Lower the aesthetic threshold by demonstrating joyful, imperfect mark-making.*

**Infrastructure**
- Chalk station boxes remain visible but unopened until end of week.
- QR signage active — confirm sidewalkcircus.org gallery is live and accepting uploads before station opens.
- Prepare chalk station for Week 3 opening: stock with blues, greens, and ochres.

**No Prompt Yet** — *Still no written prompt. The visual richness is the message.*

---

## Week 3: The Chalk Station Opens — First Invitation

**Chalk Activity**
- Open the chalk station: coloured chalk available to all walkers.
- Add the first written prompt alongside continuing decoration of steps.

**Prompt:** *"Welcome to the Palimpsest Path. What did you notice on the water today?"*

**Theory:** Low-stakes entry — observational, sensory, requires no personal disclosure. Activates the dopamine reward pathway through novelty of discovery.

**Rain Event**
- If rain clears the boards mid-week: treat as a "Fresh Start" event.
- Re-apply steps, photograph the cleared surface, re-open chalk station.
- The clearing can itself become part of the narrative — annotate it in the gallery.

> ⚠ Consider adding a chalk note after a washout: *"The rain visited. What will you leave this time?"*

---

## Week 4: Establishing Identity — The Regulars

**Prompt:** *"I have walked this track for ___ years/months. My favourite spot is ___."*

**Theory:** Activates identity construction (Saarikallio, 2011) and the dopamine reward system: walkers see their neighbours' histories appearing, creating immediate visual feedback of shared community.

**Suggested Additions**
- Add a secondary prompt near the step trail: "Add a flourish to any step you like."
- Place a few example flourishes to lower the threshold further.

---

## Week 5: Deepening Connection — The Story Ribbon

**Prompt:** *"Draw a line from your story to someone else's. What do you have in common?"*

**Theory:** Fostering social cohesion (Daykin, 2017) through physical visual links on the wood. The ribbon creates spatial evidence of connection — the boardwalk becomes a visible social network.

**Suggested Additions**
- Prompt near step trail: "What music would these steps be danced to? Write a song title."
- Add a sample ribbon in chalk yourself to model the behaviour.

**Mid-Project Check**
- Review Pico W sensor data: is dwell time increasing relative to baseline?
- Review sidewalkcircus.org gallery: what themes are emerging?
- Adjust Week 6–8 prompts if community is moving in an unexpected direction — follow the energy.

---

## Week 6: Qualitative Pulse — The Chalk Likert Scale

**Activity**
- Introduce the Chalk Likert Scale on the walkway floor.
- Photograph scales daily — upload to sidewalkcircus.org gallery with date stamp.

**Prompt:** *"How connected to Cygnet do you feel today? Mark the scale: [Isolated] ←——————→ [Connected]"*

**Data Value:** Daily photographs of the scale distribution provide a visual record of community mood over time. Aggregate marks form a qualitative time-series complementing the Pico W dwell-time data.

> ⚠ If rain clears the scale: re-draw and note the reset in the archive. A washed Likert scale is itself data — photograph the blank surface before re-seeding.

---

## Week 7: Collective Vision — Future Thinking

**Prompt:** *"What do you wish for the future of this boardwalk?"*

**Theory:** Stimulates the prefrontal cortex through future-oriented thinking and community agency. Shifts participant identity from observer to stakeholder.

**Additional Stations**
- Deploy additional chalk stations along the boardwalk for peak coverage.
- Increase chalk supply replenishment frequency.

---

## Week 8: The Grand Palimpsest — Peak Intervention

**Activity**
- All chalk stations fully stocked. Maximum visual density on the boardwalk surface.
- Multiple decoration prompts active simultaneously.

**Prompt:** *"Fill the bannister with the faces of the people you imagine walk here."*

**Theory:** Humanising parallel isolation: participants project imagined identities onto the people they share the space with but have never spoken to. The boardwalk becomes a portrait gallery of imagined community.

**Community Walk**
- Designate one day as an informal Community Walk — no formal event, just a chalked invitation.
- Prompt: "Scan the QR code to see the 8-week journey. Leave a final word for your fellow walkers."

**Termination**
- At week's end: remove chalk station boxes and QR signage.
- Do not remove chalk already applied — allow the palimpsest to fade naturally.
- Continue Pico W sensor logging for a minimum of 2 additional weeks post-termination.

> ⚠ Key post-termination question: does the habit of slowing and noticing persist after the materials are gone?

---

## Rain Contingency Protocol

Tasmanian rainfall is not a risk to be mitigated — it is a core design feature. The following protocol applies whenever a washout event removes chalk from the boardwalk surface:

- Photograph the cleared surface before any re-application. The blank palimpsest is data.
- Re-apply dance steps within 24 hours where possible.
- Log the washout date and re-seed date against the Pico W dataset. Post-washout engagement curves are a planned research outcome.
- If community-authored content is washed away, do not reproduce it — leave space for the community to re-author.
- If the Chalk Likert Scale is washed away, re-draw the scale only (not any marks). Photograph the blank scale before walkers interact.
- Multiple consecutive washout days in Weeks 1–2 may require extending those weeks by one week each. Document and note in methodology.
- If steps are washed away in Weeks 6–8 and community members spontaneously re-apply them without prompting: document carefully. This is strong evidence of habit formation.

## Sensor Data Collection Arc

The sensor array runs continuously across all phases, enabling the following analytical strata:

| Period | Stratum | Analytical Value |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| **Phase 0** | Pure Baseline | No chalk present. Establishes pre-intervention behavioural norm. |
| **Weeks 1–2** | Intervention-lite | Staged trail: footprints → tightrope → hopscotch → waltz box → colour. No prompts. Tests escalating visual stimulus alone. |
| **Weeks 3–5** | Prompt Escalation | Measures dwell response to progressive narrative depth. |
| **Weeks 6–8** | Peak Intervention | Full co-authorship. Maximum expected dwell-time divergence. |
| **Post Week 8** | Habit Persistence | Do behaviours persist after materials withdrawn? |
